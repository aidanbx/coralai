# Invest/Liquidate Softmax: Analysis and Alternatives

## What the Code Actually Does

```python
substrate.mem[:, [inds.acts_invest, inds.acts_liquidate]] = torch.softmax(
    substrate.mem[0, [inds.acts_invest, inds.acts_liquidate]], dim=0
)
```

`substrate.mem[0, [inds.acts_invest, inds.acts_liquidate]]` has shape `[2, W, H]` - two channels, one per spatial position.

`torch.softmax(..., dim=0)` normalizes across **dimension 0** - the channel dimension of size 2. So for each cell `(i, j)`:

```
acts_invest[i,j]    = exp(raw_invest[i,j])    / (exp(raw_invest[i,j]) + exp(raw_liquidate[i,j]))
acts_liquidate[i,j] = exp(raw_liquidate[i,j]) / (exp(raw_invest[i,j]) + exp(raw_liquidate[i,j]))
```

**This is per-cell normalization, not world-level.** At every cell: `acts_invest + acts_liquidate = 1`.

Now look at what `invest_liquidate` does with those values:

```python
investments  = acts_invest   * energy   # fraction of energy → infra
liquidations = acts_liquidate * infra    # fraction of infra → energy
energy += liquidations - investments
infra  += investments  - liquidations
```

At each cell: `Δenergy + Δinfra = (liquidations - investments) + (investments - liquidations) = 0`.
**Every cell conserves its own total wealth (energy + infra) exactly.** Summed globally: total world wealth is conserved by this operation every step.

---

## The Problems

### 1. There is no "do nothing"

Because `acts_invest + acts_liquidate = 1`, both are always positive. A cell with invest=0.5, liquidate=0.5, energy=10, infra=10 does:

```
investments  = 0.5 * 10 = 5
liquidations = 0.5 * 10 = 5
net change: 0
```

Fine numerically, but it just churned 5 units in each direction for nothing. If the network outputs equal logits for both (a natural "undecided" state), maximum waste occurs every step. There is no way for a cell to simply *not convert* - the minimum conversion rate is bounded away from zero by the softmax.

### 2. Magnitude is mostly lost

Because softmax is translation-invariant, `[5.0, 1.0]` and `[1.0, -3.0]` both map to the same output:

```
[0.982, 0.018]
```

The network can only control the **gap** between invest and liquidate logits. A cell that strongly wants to invest at 10% rate vs. 90% rate expresses this the same way as a cell that weakly wants it. This severely limits what the network can express - it can only encode "invest rather than liquidate," not "invest at this intensity."

### 3. The implicit homeostasis (a mixed thing)

Because `acts_invest + acts_liquidate = 1`, let `p = acts_invest`. Then:

```
Δinfra = p * energy - (1-p) * infra
       = p * (energy + infra) - infra
```

Infra grows when `p > infra / (energy + infra)` - the invest rate must *exceed the current infra fraction* of total wealth. If a cell is 90% infra, it needs p > 0.9 to keep growing infra. This creates natural resistance to runaway accumulation. It's actually somewhat elegant.

But the flip side: a cell that's 50/50 energy/infra and outputs invest=0.5 achieves zero net change with maximum unnecessary throughput. The equilibrium isn't "do nothing," it's "churn everything while staying put."

### 4. The explore softmax is doing something different - and weirder

```python
substrate.mem[0, inds.acts_explore] = torch.softmax(substrate.mem[0, inds.acts_explore], dim=0)
```

Shape is `[4, W, H]`. `dim=0` normalizes across the 4 direction channels at each cell - each cell has 4 direction values summing to 1. But then in the physics:

```python
max_act_i = torch.argmax(substrate.mem[0, inds.acts_explore], dim=0)  # just picks the winner
# ...
bid = mem[0, inds.energy, neigh_x, neigh_y]  # pays FULL energy regardless of activation value
```

The softmax values are **completely thrown away** - only the argmax direction is used, and the bid amount is hardcoded to full energy. The network can't express "explore strongly in direction X" vs "explore weakly" - only which direction it prefers. All exploration costs the same regardless of activation strength.

---

## What This Means for Evolution

The current zero-sum constraint plus forced churn has real evolutionary consequences:

- **No benefit to inaction**: Because cells always churn at nonzero rate, there's no strategy of "conserve resources." Any cell that doesn't actively manage its invest/liquidate ratio will drift.
- **Compressed action space**: Since only the gap between invest and liquidate logits matters (not magnitude), the effective dimensionality of the investment decision is 1 - a scalar preference, not a rate. NEAT can't evolve "invest at 5%" vs "invest at 50%" as distinct strategies.
- **The economy isn't really an economy**: Wealth is conserved per cell per step (minus noise injection). Cells don't gain or lose - they rearrange between energy and infra. The real source of wealth creation is external noise; death is the only real sink. Competition is about *positioning* (who has enough infra to attract energy via `energy_physics`) rather than *economic behavior*.

## More Natural Alternatives

The core issue is that the softmax collapses continuous preferences into allocation fractions that lose intent and prevent inaction.

### Option 1: Independent Sigmoid Gates

```python
invest_rate    = torch.sigmoid(raw_invest)    * MAX_INVEST_RATE
liquidate_rate = torch.sigmoid(raw_liquidate) * MAX_LIQUIDATE_RATE

investments  = invest_rate * energy
liquidations = liquidate_rate * infra
```

Cells can simultaneously invest 20% and liquidate 5%, or do nothing (both near zero), or both at full rate. To make zero logit mean near-zero activity (rather than 50%), shift: `sigmoid(raw - 3)` gives ~0.05 at zero input.

### Option 2: Single Signed Trade Channel (the most natural)

```python
trade_signal = torch.tanh(raw_trade)          # ∈ (-1, 1)
max_rate     = 0.3
investments  = relu(trade_signal)  * max_rate * energy
liquidations = relu(-trade_signal) * max_rate * infra
```

One degree of freedom per cell: positive = invest, negative = liquidate, zero = do nothing. Magnitude expresses intensity. This directly maps to biology: anabolism vs catabolism on a single axis. Cuts the action space in half and restores magnitude meaning to the network's output.

### Option 3: Budget-Aware Sigmoid

```python
invest_drive  = torch.sigmoid(raw_invest)
max_invest    = torch.clamp(energy / energy_threshold, 0, 1)
max_liquidate = torch.clamp(infra  / infra_threshold,  0, 1)

investments  = invest_drive       * max_invest   * energy * dt
liquidations = (1 - invest_drive) * max_liquidate * infra  * dt
```

Conversion is naturally bounded by available substrate - you can't invest more energy than you have. The Michaelis-Menten half-saturation form (`energy / (Km + energy)`) is the classic kinetics version of this, saturating at high substrate concentration.

### Option 4: Keep Softmax but Break the Zero-Sum

The zero-sum property (`Δenergy + Δinfra = 0`) comes from the symmetric exchange, not the softmax. Add entropy:

```python
efficiency   = 0.9   # or evolve this, or tie to com channels
investments  = acts_invest   * energy * efficiency
liquidations = acts_liquidate * infra  * efficiency
```

Now converting energy to infra and back loses a fraction. Cells that cycle wastefully degrade. Creates thermodynamic pressure toward purposeful economic behavior. Efficiency could be a per-genome evolved parameter or even a spatially varying environmental field.

## Summary Table

| Approach | "Do nothing" | Magnitude | Zero-sum | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Current (softmax) | No - always churns | Lost (gap only) | Yes | Churn at equilibrium |
| Independent sigmoid | Yes (near-zero) | Preserved | No | Most flexible |
| Tanh trade (signed) | Yes (exactly 0) | Preserved | No | Simplest, most natural |
| Budget-aware sigmoid | Yes | Preserved | No | Physically grounded |
| Softmax + efficiency loss | No - still churns | Lost | No | Minimal change, adds entropy |

The most impactful single change would be **Option 2** (tanh trade channel): it halves the action space, allows true inaction, restores magnitude meaning, and has a clean biological analogy in anabolism/catabolism balance.
